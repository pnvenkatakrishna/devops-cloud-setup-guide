# AWS Free Tier Account Creation Guide

## Prerequisites

- Valid Email Address
- Mobile Number for OTP Verification
- Debit/Credit Card with **International Transactions Enabled**
- Stable Internet Connection

---

## Step 1: Open AWS Free Tier Website

Open the AWS Free Tier page:

https://aws.amazon.com/free/

Click **Create a Free Account**.

> Screenshot: Step-01

---

## Step 2: Enter Account Information

Provide the following details:

- Email Address
- AWS Account Name

Click **Verify Email Address**.

> Screenshot: Step-02

---

## Step 3: Verify Email Address

- Check your email inbox.
- Enter the verification code received from AWS.
- Click **Verify**.

> Screenshot: Step-03

---

## Step 4: Create Root User Password

Create a strong password and click **Continue**.

> Screenshot: Step-04

---

## Step 5: Select Account Type

Choose:

- Personal

Enter:

- Full Name
- Phone Number
- Country/Region
- Address Details

Click **Continue**.

> Screenshot: Step-05

---

## Step 6: Add Payment Information

Enter:

- Cardholder Name
- Card Number
- Expiry Date
- CVV

Ensure the following are enabled on your card:

- International Transactions
- Online/E-commerce Transactions

Click **Verify and Continue**.

> Screenshot: Step-06

---

## Step 7: Identity Verification

- Enter Mobile Number
- Complete CAPTCHA Verification
- Click **Send SMS**
- Enter the OTP received on your mobile

> Screenshot: Step-07

---

## Step 8: Select Support Plan

Choose:

- Basic Support (Free)

Click **Complete Sign Up**.

> Screenshot: Step-08

---

## Step 9: Account Activation

AWS validates your information and activates the account.

Activation may take a few minutes and, in some cases, up to 24 hours.

> Screenshot: Step-09

---

## Step 10: Sign In to AWS Console

Open:

https://console.aws.amazon.com/

Select:

- Root User
- Enter Email Address
- Enter Password

Click **Sign In**.

> Screenshot: Step-10

---

## Post-Creation Recommendations

### Enable MFA

Secure the root account using Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA).

### Create an IAM User

Avoid using the root account for daily activities.

Create an IAM user with appropriate permissions and use it for regular work.

### Configure Billing Alerts

Create a billing budget and email notifications to monitor AWS usage and avoid unexpected charges.
